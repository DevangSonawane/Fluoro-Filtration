### Who We Are
We're a dedicated distributor of industrial liquid filtration products, serving manufacturers, processors, and facilities that need dependable filtration without the guesswork. Rather than pushing a single product line, we match the right filter media, cartridge, or housing to your specific application — every time.

We work across filter media, liquid filter bags, cartridges (melt blown, string wound, pleated, membrane), high-flow units, and housings — covering water treatment, food & beverage, pharmaceuticals, chemical processing, oil & gas, electronics, and power generation.

### What We Do
Before we recommend a product, we look at the details that actually determine performance:

- **Micron rating** — what needs to be removed, and how fine
- **Chemical compatibility** — what the media needs to withstand over its service life
- **Flow rate** — sizing the housing and cartridge count to your throughput
- **Operating conditions** — temperature, pressure, and differential pressure limits

Getting these four right the first time avoids premature filter failure, unplanned downtime, and wasted spend on the wrong product.

### How We Work
Not sure whether a pleated cartridge or a string-wound filter is the better fit for a high-viscosity fluid? Unsure what housing material will hold up against your process chemistry? That's exactly where we come in.

Send us your process details — fluid type, flow rate, particulate load, temperature, and pressure — and we'll narrow the options to what will actually work, before you commit to a quote.

### Operating Guidance
Use the product family spec pages for detailed temperature, pressure, and media selection guidance specific to each cartridge or bag type. General operating envelopes across our range:

- **Melt blown / string wound cartridges:** up to 120°C depending on core material, up to 2.0 bar max DP
- **Pleated PP/glass fiber cartridges:** up to 80°C, up to 3 bar max DP
- **Membrane cartridges (PES/PTFE/PVDF/Nylon 6):** up to 100°C depending on media, up to 4.8 bar max DP
- **Filter bags:** filtration pressure 1.03–1.72 kg/sq.cm, 130–160°C depending on media
